About — Hasan Khan
Hasan Khan
> Software Engineer Intern @ NVIDIA
📍 Santa Clara, CA

I'm a software engineer who likes building things that live close to the hardware. Right now I'm at NVIDIA on the DGX Mission Control team, writing automated hardware-recovery tooling that keeps AI-factory infrastructure healthy across GB200, GB300, and Vera Rubin NVL72 systems. Before this I built developer tooling at SAP and a financial-markets frontend at the Bank of Canada, with earlier internships spanning data, security, and research at Accenture, GeoComply, and the University of Alberta.

Day to day I write Python, Go, C++, and TypeScript, and I'm comfortable moving between low-level systems code and the product layer on top. I got into security through Capture the Flag competitions and still play them when I can; chasing flags has made me a sharper debugger everywhere else. Mostly I care about software that's fast, reliable, and not annoying to use.

Outside of work I co-run Kuudra Gang, a Minecraft Discord community that's grown past 45,000 members, where I run the bots, automation, and the day-to-day moderation. I like shipping, taking things apart to understand them, and the odd 2am hackathon. If you're working on something ambitious, a CTF, or a hackathon, my inbox is open.
